Works of Vincent Van Gogh

The Starry Night Vincent Van Gogh painted Starry Night in 1889 during his stay at the asylum of Saint-Paul-de-Masusole near Saint-Remy-de-Provence. Van Gogh lived well in the hospital; he was allowed more freedoms than any other patients. If attended, he could leave the hospital grounds; he was allowed to paint, read, and withdraw into his own room.
Sunflowers Van Gogh's paintings of Sunflowers are among his most famous. He did them in Arles, in south of France, in 1888 and 1889. Vincent painted a total of five large canvases with sunflowers in a vase, with three shades of yellow 'and nothing else'. In the way, he demostrated that it was possible to create an image with numerous variations of a single color, without any loss of eloquence.
