Buisness Etiquette

As you have likely been told time and time again, it takes only ten or 15 seconds to make a first imprestion and the rest of your life to undo it. With practice, you can learn to present yourself as a polished professional every time you meet someone. Here are some guidlines to get you off on the right foot.

You five Buisness Introduction Rules

    1. Stand Up: When you are selcted and someone approaches you to say "hello" with a handshake, stand up, whether you are a man or a woman. By doing so, you show the person you are greating that they are worth your full attention.
    2. Make Eye Contact; By looking someone directly in the eye, you accomplish three things
      • You will project an image of self-confindence and a healthy self-esteem.
      • You will be peceived as a good listener.
      • You will probably recive the same courtesy when you speak
    3. Smile: It sends others a message of acceptance.
    4. Say Your Name: If you are metting someone for the firs time you should introduce yourself with your first and last name.
    5. Shake HandsGenerally speaking, a handshake should last only for a as long as it take to greet the person. It should be firm enough to display your sense of confidence without being a bonecrusher.
  • Sloppy Language:No matter how well you dress, using sloppy language will lesson your credibility with others. For example saying "yeah" instead of "yes", or using profanity, especially around people that would not find that impressive. Most people consider your choice of words a reflection of your intelligence
  • Giggling: While laughter cam be the best medicine for many ills, giggling can be annoying.
  • Hiding your Hands: Believe it or not, etiquette reseach has shown that hands above board are more apperling thab hands hidden away in pockets. When your hands are not showing, it often sends signals to somone that you are not telling all of that you are holding somhing back.
  • Gum-Chewing: Let there be no doubt about it, you should not chew gum when meeting people. No exceptions.
  • Throat Clearing: The habit of clearing your throat several times during a converstaion can be very distracting
